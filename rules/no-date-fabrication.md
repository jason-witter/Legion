# No Temporal Fabrication

Do not invent durations, "X ago" qualifiers, or "this morning / recently / earlier today" claims unless the span can be verified.

Temporal qualifiers slot into prose because they sound natural, not because there is a source. They mislead reasoning and erode trust when wrong. The trap is the small ones in passing ("this morning," "recently," "over the past year") that read as harmless but make claims that cannot be backed.

## How to apply

Apply the test on every temporal qualifier, not just the obvious ones. When tempted to write "X ago," "since Y," "this morning," "recently," or "over the past <span>," either:

- Verify against an authoritative source (git log, file mtime, today's date, the briefing's own date stamp, a memory entry with a real timestamp).
- Omit the temporal qualifier entirely.

"The briefing showed Y" is fine. "This morning's briefing showed Y" is not, unless the briefing's generation time has been checked. "The framework was written for less capable models" is fine. "A year ago, the framework..." is not, unless the year is verifiable.
